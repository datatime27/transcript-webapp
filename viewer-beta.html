Intro

  • This webpage loads the YouTube episode alongside the transcript. I have produced the transcript using a process that estimates who is speaking. However, in practice, the estimations are only correct some of the time.

Getting started

  • The process I used to estimate speakers doesn't know speaker names, so you'll just see SPEAKER_00, SPEAKER_01, etc.
  • The first time you change a SPEAKER_ to a real name the tool will ask you if you'd like to apply this to all other instances of that speaker as well.
  • Be warned sometimes the tool gets the speaker wrong (especially during the intro theme), so check that a given speaker seems correct before applying to all of them.
  • In practice I found it's best to start with the prize task. Then each of the 7 speakers gets a turn at speaking, which will give you a better idea of who is who.
  • There is also the Other speaker which is for anything that can't be attributed to one of the 7 speakers: (e.g.: applause, noises, guest speaker)

Playback

  • Space Play / pause
  • ↑ ↓ Previous / next caption
  • Click caption text or timecode — seek and play
  • Click speaker button — seek and pause, open speaker picker
  • The active caption is highlighted in red.

Assigning speakers

  • For each line you select the speaker from the drop down menu.
  • For each group of lines from the same speaker there is also a group header that you can select to change the speaker for all lines.
  • 1–9 Assign the nth speaker to the active caption
  • ` The backtick key (the key to the left of 1 on the keyboard) assigns the previous different speaker to the active caption.
  • So if you have a scene where two speakers are talking back and forth, the system will sometimes assign all of the lines to just one speaker. As you play through the scene, you can press the backtick key in order to set the active line to the other speaker.
  • Manually edited captions are highlighted in green so you can track your progress.

Saving

  • Click Save (top-right) to save your progress. Each save creates a new version.
  • Currently, there is no undo — double-check before using the Apply to all feature.
  • You can switch episodes from the dropdown in the header; you'll be warned if there are unsaved changes.

Settings ⚙

  • Click ⚙ (top-right) to open settings.
  • Scroll delay — how long to wait before the transcript auto-scrolls to the active caption.
  • Speaker colors — customize the color for each speaker slot. Changes take effect immediately and are saved for your next visit.

Known limitations: rapid back-and-forth banter and live studio tasks tend to have more speaker errors. Speaker boundaries are sometimes off by one caption. Timecodes are mostly in sync but may drift slightly in some spots.
